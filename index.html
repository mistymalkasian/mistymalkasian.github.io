About me

Turning a childhood hobby into a new career

I've been fiddling with HTML and CSS since I was 12 years old. Several sites let kids customize their own user lookups, etc. and I loved to do that. I also liked to read through other people's code and try to work out what each line was doing. I would endlessly change and tweak things and see what happened on the screen as a result.

That fascination with the way computers speak has carried over into my adult life. When I saw that "coding bootcamps" were a thing, I eagerly looked into attending one, and when that became a possibility for me, I jumped right in. I graduated devCodeCamp Milwaukee in December 2017, learning how to code in only 3 months. As a result I have expanded on my HTML and CSS knowledge and added JavaScript, C#, and ASP.NET MVC to the list of languages I am fluent in. I use GitHub as my source control and have also used it to collaborate with others on projects.

I'm a self-motivated team player who is enthusiastic about becoming the best at what I do. I find programming, whether back end or front end, fun and rewarding.

Interests

In my spare time, I love to read, write, and edit. Editing skills also come in handy when I'm programming because the source of any exception or error is easier to spot when I'm already constantly on the lookout for mistakes.

I've always enjoyed playing computer or console games in my free time as well. I eventually want to learn how these games are programmed and try my hand at making one myself.
